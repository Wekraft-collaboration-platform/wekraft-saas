# Profile & Settings

Your Wekraft profile is your identity across the platform. It's visible to teammates in Team Space, project cards, and task assignments. Keep it updated so your team knows who you are and what you bring to the table.

## Accessing Your Profile

You can get to your profile in two ways:

1. **Sidebar Menu** — click your avatar at the bottom of the sidebar and select **"Profile"**
2. **Direct URL** — navigate to `/dashboard/my-profile`

---

## Profile Information

### Basic Details

| Field | Description | Editable? |
|---|---|---|
| **Full Name** | Your display name shown across the platform (comments, assignments, Team Space) | ✓ |
| **Username** | A unique handle (e.g., `@ritesh`). Used in mentions and profile URLs | ✓ |
| **Email** | Your registered email address (from your sign-in provider) | Read-only |
| **Avatar** | Profile photo — upload a custom image or use auto-generated initials | ✓ |

### Professional Details

| Field | Description | Editable? |
|---|---|---|
| **Occupation** | Your job title (e.g., "Senior Frontend Developer") | ✓ |
| **Identity** | Student, Professional, or Freelancer | ✓ |
| **Role** | Developer, Designer, Project Manager, Product Owner, or Other | ✓ |
| **Bio** | A short description about yourself — visible in Team Space profiles | ✓ |

### Skills

Add tags for your technical and professional skills (e.g., `React`, `Node.js`, `UI/UX Design`, `Project Management`). Skills serve two purposes:

- They appear on your **profile card in Team Space**, helping team leads understand your expertise
- They help project owners **assign the right tasks** to the right people based on skill match

To add skills, type the skill name and press Enter. You can add as many as you like.

### Social Links

Connect your profiles from other platforms to make it easy for teammates to find you:

| Platform | What to enter |
|---|---|
| **GitHub** | Your GitHub username (e.g., `riteshsinha`) |
| **LinkedIn** | Your full LinkedIn profile URL |
| **Twitter/X** | Your Twitter handle (e.g., `@ritesh`) |
| **Portfolio** | Your personal website URL |

Social links appear when someone clicks into your full profile from Team Space or a project member list.

## Account Settings

### Connected Accounts

Your profile page shows which authentication providers are linked to your Wekraft account:

| Provider | Used for |
|---|---|
| **GitHub** | Login + repository linking + commit stats on dashboard |
| **Google** | Login only |

If GitHub isn't connected, the dashboard's Commits and PR metric cards will show **"Connect Now"** prompts. Connecting GitHub enables:
- Commit and PR stats on your main dashboard
- Repository linking for projects
- File Explorer access
- GitHub Issue importing

### Plan & Usage

Your profile page displays your current subscription plan and live usage:

| Metric | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Current Plan** | Free, Plus, or Pro — with an upgrade link |
| **Projects Owned** | Count vs. plan limit (e.g., `2/20`) |
| **Projects Joined** | Count vs. plan limit |

To change your plan, click **"Upgrade"** or visit the [Pricing page](/dashboard/pricing).

## Privacy

Your profile information is visible to:

| Data | Who can see it |
|---|---|
| **Name & Avatar** | Anyone in a shared project (Team Space, comments, task assignments) |
| **Public project visibility** | If you're in a public project, your name and avatar are visible to anyone viewing the project |
| **Bio, Skills, Social Links** | Visible only when someone clicks into your full profile |
| **Email address** | **Never** shared publicly — only visible to you |

---

## Best Practices

- **Use a real photo** — it helps teammates recognize you in comments, assignments, and Team Space
- **Keep skills updated** — accurate skills help project leads assign the right tasks to the right people
- **Add a bio** — a one-liner about what you're working on gives context to new team members
- **Connect GitHub** — enables repository linking, issue importing, commit tracking, and dashboard stats
- **Fill in social links** — helps teammates find you on GitHub for code reviews, or on LinkedIn for professional context
